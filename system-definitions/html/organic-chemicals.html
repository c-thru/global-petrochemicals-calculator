Air and Water in the outputs represent unreacted oxygen and water that are wasted, WasteOtherChemicals represents unreacted ethylene. The quantities of these outputs were calculated based on a process yield of 75.3%, reported by [Tecnon OrbiChem, 2016]. This yield is the ratio of actual Ethylene required to produce EthyleneGlycol and the theoretical quantity.

The calculations behind this recipe are in an accompanying spreadsheet.

A few additional “objects” need to be defined for the calculator model:

Object: HydrogenCyanide

Hydrogen cyanide is consumed to produce hexamethylenediamine

Object: IsophthalicAcid

Isophthalic acid is consumed to produce polyphthalamide

Object: TolueneDiisocyanate

TolueneDiisocyanate is consumed to produce polyurethane

Object: PropyleneOxide

Propylene oxide is consumed to produce polyols

Object: EthyleneOxide

Ethylene oxide is consumed to produce polyols

Object: Polyols

Polyols is consumed to produce polyurethane
