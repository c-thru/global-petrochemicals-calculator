3. Primary chemicals#

These processes produce primary chemicals such as ethylene and BTX.

3.1. Primary chemicals#

Most of the main chemicals are already defined from the UK FIRES production system map [Lupton et al., 2024], reproduced here for completeness:

3.2. Fossil feedstocks#

Steam cracking produces olefins (ethylene, propylene) from a range of fossil feedstocks. Two feedstocks are modelled, ethane (which also represents other lighter feeds such as LPG) and naphtha (which also represents other heavier feeds).

3.3. From methyl alcohol#

Collectively, these processes are known as methanol-to-X (MTX). Methanol production processes are defined in Methyl alcohol.
